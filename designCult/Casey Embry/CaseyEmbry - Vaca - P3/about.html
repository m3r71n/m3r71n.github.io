History

St.Kitts and Nevis have been a beautiful home to many for thousands of years. When Christopher Columbus arrived in the Americas he called the islands St. Christopher and Nieves. After the patron saint Christopher and the Spanish word for snow nieves. Over time the slands came to be called St. Kitts and Nevis. St. Kitts was the first English colony in the Caribbean in 1683. The English and French would feud over the islands for years before the English took it as their colony. The British Empire used the island as a grounds to grow sugar cane. The British brought in large numbers of Africans to serve as slaves on sugar cane plantations until 1834 when slavery was abolished by Parliament. The island remained under the United Kingdom until 1983, when St. Kitts and Nevs gained independence from the United Kingdom. The island used sugar production as it's main economic product until 2005 when prices dropped greatly. The isalnd now has a booming tourism for great people like you!
